Raspberry Pi Zero and Zero W Quick Start Guide

Byadmin

Mar 6, 2020

Connecting your Raspberry Pi

Micro USB Port (labeled USB)
  • Connect a USB OTG micro B to a USB A cable adapter to be able to connect the USB devices.
  • Both the Zero and Zero W have only one micro USB port for peripheral devices. We recommend using the Mini Wireless USB Keyboard with Touchpad (available at www.argon40.com.br) or any similar device.
  • You may use a powered USB hub to expand your ports in case you plan to connect other devices to your Zero/Zero W.
Mini HDMI Port
  • You may connect a standard HDMI cable to your Pi and monitor.
Micro SD Card Port
  • Insert a pre-loaded micro SD card into the Pi (either a NOOBS or a fully supported operating system). See https://www.raspberrypi.org/documentation/ on how to prepare a new Micro SD card (NOOBS).
  • If you purchased the Argon 40 Raspberry Pi Zero Starter Kit, you can just plug in the Micro SD card with pre-installed NOOBS.
4 GPIO Headers
  • The Pi Zero and Zero W GPIO headers [1] are unpopulated.
  • You can also use a 2×20 male header or a 2×20 female socket header. These come with the Argon40 Raspberry Pi Basic and Starter Kit. They are available at www.argon40.com.br.

[1] For a more detailed explanation of the GPIO, please read here.

5 Micro USB Port 2 (Labeled PWR IN)
  • Connect a 5V, 1.5Amp power supply to power up your Raspberry Pi Zero/Zero W.

Getting Started with your Raspberry Pi Zero and Zero W

*Note: you may skip steps 1 & 2 if you have the SD card that comes with the Argon40 Starter Kit, as it has Raspberry Pi NOOBS pre-installed.

1. Download NOOBS and format SD card

1.1 Download the NOOBS zip file for offline or network installation from http://www.raspberrypi.org/downloads on your computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux).
1.2 Extract the ZIP files to a folder on your computer.
1.3 Format your SD card using the SD Association’s SD Formatter 4.0 for either Windows or Mac. Refer to https://www.sdcard.org/downloads/formatter_4/ for more details.

2. NOOBS files installation

2.1 Drag all the files in the extracted NOOBS folder and drop them into the SD card drive.
2.2 When the transfer is finished, remove your SD card from your computer and insert it into your Raspberry Pi. Plug in your keyboard, mouse, monitor cables, and the SD card with NOOBS.

2.3 Plug in the USB power cable to your Raspberry Pi Zero or Zero W.
2.4 Your Raspberry Pi will boot, and a window will appear with a list of different operating systems that you can install. We recommend the Raspbian, a version of Linux’s “Debian” operating system. Tick the box next to it and click to Install.
2.5 Raspbian will then begin installing! (Although, this may take a while!)

3 First boot and accessing the graphical user interface

3.1 When installation has completed, you may set the Pi’s configuration in the tab: Menu > Preferences > Raspberry Pi Configuration menu (raspi-config).
3.2 For other settings the default ROOT login of your raspberry pi are as follows:

username: pi
password: raspberry

Enjoy your Raspberry Pi Zero/Raspberry Pi Zero W!
