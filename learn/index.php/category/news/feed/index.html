  • CPU: A Broadcom BCM2835 application, with a 1GHz ARM11 core
  • Memory: 512MB of LPDDR2 SDRAM
  • Storage: 1 x micro-SD card slot
  • Video out: 1 x mini-HDMI socket for 1080p60 video output (needs mini-HDMI-HDMI adapter)
  • Ports: 1 x USB On The Go port (needs USB OTG to USB adapter), micro USB port for power.
  • Expansion: An unpopulated 40-pin GPIO header with identical pinout to Model A+/B+. An unpopulated composite video header, CSI camera connector
  • Size: 65mm x 30mm x 5mm
  • Weight: 9g

What is the difference between the Raspberry Pi Zero and Raspberry Pi Zero W?

The only difference between the Pi Zero and Pi Zero W is that the Pi Zero W has

  1. Bluetooth 4.1
  2. Bluetooth Low Energy
  3. 802.11 b/g/n Wireless LAN

Ports of Raspberry Pi Zero and Raspberry Pi Zero W?

  • Ports: 1 x USB On The Go port (needs USB OTG to USB adapter), micro USB port for power.
  • Expansion: An unpopulated 40-pin GPIO header with identical pinout to Model A+/B+. An unpopulated composite video header, CSI camera connector

Why can’t I purchase more than one at a time?

Because we want as many people as possible to get a chance to purchase the Raspberry Pi Zero and Zero W.

Why are there packs sometimes with the Pi Zero W even when there aren’t any Pi Zero Ws in stock?

We want to try to balance the demand on kits and individual boards. The Pi Zero W really is a barebones computer and you would definitely need accessories to make projects and use the Pi Zero to its full potential.
